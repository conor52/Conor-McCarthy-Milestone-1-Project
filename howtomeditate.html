Step 1: Start your meditation

  • Preferabely, it's slightly better to have a cushion laid out on the floor to sit on.
  • Sit with your legs crossed if you're able or any position thats relatively comfortable, but not too comfortable or you'll be prone to falling asleep/
  • Dont worry, sitting on a chair is perfectly acceptable too! Dont bother learning any advanced lotus leg crossing, it's more likely to cause harm than good.

Step 2: Close your eyes

  • To start off, simply follow your breath on its inhaliation and exhalation. Over time, this will build concentration.
  • Watch out for distractions between breaths. Hold the intention to not let your mind get caught up in whatever it throws at you.
  • Set a goal to count say 10 breaths without getting distracted. Once you get the hang of that, see if you can slowly increase this number. Try increase from say 10 breaths, to 15, after that 20 etc.

Step 3: Common Pitfalls

  • If you start getting angry at yourself for getting so distracted and mind wandering, realise that you are in fact still meditating as long as you shift your attention back to the breath afterwards. Being angry at yourself is actually really counter productive.
  • Meditating every singe day is key here. There is no such bad thing as a bad meditation session. They only bad thing you can possibly do is skip a session out of laziness.
